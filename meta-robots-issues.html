Critical Issue

Meta Robots Index Tag Problems

Incorrect meta robots tags are preventing important pages from being indexed or allowing low-value pages to be indexed by search engines.

Site-Specific Observations

Observation: An audit of the site's meta robots tags revealed multiple configuration errors that directly impact indexability and search visibility.

Issue Assessment

Issue Priority Rationale
Category Pages Set to Index Critical Category archive pages are currently set to index, follow but should be noindex, follow. These pages are thin and compete with main content; keeping them indexable wastes crawl budget and creates duplicate content issues.
Demo Page Live in Production Critical /demo-2/ should not be present on the live site. It should be deleted or kept as a draft. Leaving test/demo pages accessible creates a poor user experience and may be indexed accidentally.
Multiple Thank-You Pages Indexed Critical Thank-you pages (/thank-you/, /thank-you-2/) are set to index. These utility pages provide no SEO value and should be set to noindex, follow to prevent them from appearing in search results.
Meta Form Page Is Thin Content Warning /meta-form/ contains very little unique content. Either delete this page if it is no longer needed, or apply noindex, follow to prevent it from entering the search index.
Calendly Booking Page Indexed Warning /calendly-booking-thank-you/ is a post-conversion utility page with minimal content. It should be set to noindex, follow so it does not compete with valuable pages in search results.

Recommendations

1

Change Category Pages to Noindex

Update all category archive pages from index, follow to noindex, follow. Category pages are typically thin and create internal competition with main content pages.

2

Delete or Draft the Demo Page

Remove /demo-2/ from the live site entirely, or keep it as a draft if it is still being worked on. If it must remain live temporarily, apply noindex, follow immediately.

3

Noindex All Thank-You Pages

Apply noindex, follow to /thank-you/ and /thank-you-2/. Post-conversion pages should never appear in search results as they provide no value to organic visitors.

4

Fix Thin and Utility Pages

Either delete /meta-form/ if it is no longer needed, or set it to noindex, follow. Do the same for /calendly-booking-thank-you/ to keep the index clean and preserve crawl budget.

The Importance of Meta Robots Tags

1

Indexability Control

Meta robots tags are the primary on-page mechanism for telling search engines whether a page should be included in their index. A single incorrect noindex directive can make an entire page invisible to organic search, regardless of its content quality or backlink profile.

2

Crawl Budget Management

Search engines allocate a limited crawl budget to each site. By noindexing low-value pages, you ensure crawlers spend more time on your important content rather than wasting resources on thank-you pages, duplicates, and thin content.

3

Preventing Index Bloat

When low-quality or irrelevant pages are indexed, they dilute your site's overall quality signals. Proper use of meta robots tags keeps the index clean, focused on valuable content, and improves the site's perceived authority and relevance.

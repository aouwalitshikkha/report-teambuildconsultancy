Critical Issue

Page with Redirect

Multiple pages are redirecting to the homepage or irrelevant pages, wasting crawl budget and confusing users.

Site-Specific Observations

Observation: An audit of internal redirects revealed numerous pages that redirect to the homepage or unrelated content instead of their logical destination.

Issue Assessment

Source URL Redirects To Priority Rationale
/careers/ Homepage Critical A careers page should remain accessible or redirect to a relevant hiring page. Redirecting to the homepage frustrates job seekers and wastes any existing backlinks.
/2026/01/26/ Homepage Critical Date archive pages redirecting to the homepage serve no purpose. If the archive is not needed, it should return a 404 or be properly redirected to the blog index.
/benefits-of-hiring-a-remote-xero-bookkeeper/ Blog page (irrelevant) Critical A specific article about Xero bookkeepers redirecting to a generic blog page breaks user intent. Visitors expecting specific content land on an unrelated page, increasing bounce rate.

Recommendations

1

Only Keep Relevant Redirects

Maintain 301 redirects only when the source and destination are contextually related. For example, an old service page should redirect to the updated version of that same service — never to the homepage or an unrelated section.

2

Remove Homepage Redirects

Stop redirecting removed pages to the homepage by default. If a page is gone and has no relevant replacement, serve a proper 404 response. This tells search engines the content is permanently gone and preserves user trust.

3

Audit and Fix Irrelevant Redirects

Review all redirects mapped to generic pages (like the main blog index). Point each URL to the most relevant existing content, or remove the redirect and let the URL return 404 if no suitable replacement exists.

The Importance of Proper Redirects

1

Preserve Link Equity

Backlinks pointing to redirected pages pass authority to the destination. When everything redirects to the homepage, that equity is diluted across a generic page rather than strengthening relevant content. Proper redirects ensure link value flows to the right place.

2

User Intent and Experience

Users click a link with a specific expectation. Landing on the homepage or an unrelated page creates frustration and increases bounce rate. Relevant redirects respect user intent and keep visitors engaged with your site.

3

Search Engine Clarity

Search engines treat mass homepage redirects as soft 404s. This wastes crawl budget and creates confusion about which pages are actually available. Clear, relevant redirects or proper 404s help crawlers understand your site structure accurately.

Recommendations

1

Remove Category Sitemap from Index

Exclude the category sitemap entirely from the sitemap index. Since category archive pages are noindex, they should not be submitted to search engines via sitemap.

2

Remove Redirected Testimonial Pages

Audit and remove all testimonial URLs that return 301 or 302 redirects from the sitemap. Replace them with the final destination URLs if those pages should be indexed.

3

Exclude Thin Talents Pages

Remove talents profile pages from the sitemap unless they contain substantial unique content. Consider consolidating thin profiles or adding noindex to low-value pages to prevent index bloat.

4

Purge Unnecessary Utility Pages

Remove the following non-indexable utility pages from the page sitemap: /demo-2/, /thank-you/, /calendly-booking-thank-you/, /thank-you-2/, and /meta-form/. Add noindex tags to these pages if not already present.

The Importance of XML Sitemaps

1

Discovery and Indexing

An XML sitemap acts as a roadmap for search engines, helping them discover and understand the structure of your website. It ensures that all important pages — especially those without strong internal linking — are found and considered for indexing.

2

Crawl Efficiency

Sitemaps provide metadata about each URL, such as last modified date and update frequency. This helps search engines prioritize crawling, allocate crawl budget more effectively, and revisit updated content faster.

3

Large and Complex Sites

For websites with deep architecture, thousands of pages, or dynamic content, a sitemap is essential. Without it, search engine crawlers may miss newly added or deeply nested pages, leading to incomplete indexing.
